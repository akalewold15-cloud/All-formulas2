Mathematics Hub

Quadratic Formula Grade 10

x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a

Used to find the roots of a quadratic equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0.

Area of a Circle Grade 9

A = πr²

Used to calculate the total area inside a circle, where r is the radius and π ≈ 3.14159.

Pythagorean Theorem Grade 9

a² + b² = c²

In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (c) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides (a, b).

Law of Sines Grade 11/12

sin(A)/a = sin(B)/b = sin(C)/c

Relates the lengths of the sides of any triangle to the sines of its angles.

Derivative of xⁿ Grade 12

d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹

Basic rule for finding the derivative of a power function in Calculus.

Physics Hub

Newton's Second Law of Motion Grade 9

F = ma

The force (F) acting on an object is equal to the mass (m) of that object times its acceleration (a).

Kinematics Equations Grade 9-11

v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as

Equations for uniform acceleration, where u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, s = displacement, t = time.

Ohm's Law Grade 10

V = IR

The voltage (V) across a conductor is directly proportional to the current (I) flowing through it, where R is the resistance.

Einstein's Mass-Energy Equivalence Grade 11/12

E = mc²

Mass (m) can be converted into energy (E), where c is the speed of light (~3×10⁸ m/s).

Snell's Law of Refraction Grade 10/11

n₁ sin(θ₁) = n₂ sin(θ₂)

Describes the relationship between the angles of incidence and refraction for a wave passing between two different media.

Chemistry Hub

Concentrated vs. Dilute Solutions Grade 10/11

Feature Concentrated Solution Dilute Solution
Solvent Amount Small (Little water) Large (Lots of water)
Solute Amount High (Lots of substance) Low (Little substance)
pH level (Acids) Very low (Strong Acid) Higher (Weak Acid)
Safety Highly Corrosive (Danger) Less Corrosive (Safer)

The Mole Concept Grade 10

n = m / M

Used to calculate the number of moles (n), where m is the mass (grams) and M is the molar mass (g/mol).

Ideal Gas Law Grade 11

PV = nRT

Relates pressure (P), volume (V), moles (n), and temperature (T) for an ideal gas, where R is the gas constant.

pH Calculation Grade 11/12

pH = -log[H⁺]

pH is the measure of hydrogen ion concentration [H⁺] in a solution, determining its acidity or alkalinity.

Biology Hub

Cellular Respiration (Aerobic) Grade 10/11

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy (ATP)

Process by which cells break down glucose (sugar) in the presence of oxygen to produce energy.

Photosynthesis Grade 10/11

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Process used by plants to convert light energy into chemical energy (glucose) using carbon dioxide and water.

Simpson's Diversity Index Grade 11/12

D = 1 - [Σn(n-1) / N(N-1)]

A measure of species diversity in an ecological community, where n is the total number of organisms of a particular species, and N is the total number of organisms of all species.

Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium Grade 12

p² + 2pq + q² = 1

Predicts how gene frequencies will be inherited from one generation to the next given certain conditions, where p and q are allele frequencies.
